# Wing Chun Teen Curriculum: Self-Discipline and Awareness Program

## Program Philosophy

This curriculum is designed for teenagers aged 13-18, focusing on developing essential life skills through the principles of Wing Chun Kung Fu. Our program emphasizes:

- **Self-Discipline**: Building consistent practice habits, respect for instructors and peers, and personal responsibility.
- **Respect**: Cultivating mutual respect, humility, and ethical behavior both inside and outside the training hall.
- **Street Awareness**: Teaching situational awareness, conflict de‑escalation, and practical self‑defense strategies that prioritize avoidance and escape over confrontation.

## Curriculum Structure

The curriculum is organized into progressive modules, each combining physical training, mental conditioning, and real‑world application.

1. **Foundations of Awareness** – Core principles of situational awareness, basic Wing Chun stances, and introductory sensitivity drills.
2. **Structure and Control** – Building posture, centerline theory, and controlled contact exercises.
3. **Practical Self‑Defense** – Applying Wing Chun techniques to common threat scenarios, with emphasis on legal and ethical boundaries.
4. **Confidence and Leadership** – Developing assertiveness, decision‑making under pressure, and peer mentoring.

## Teaching Methodology

- **Age‑Appropriate Drills**: All exercises are adapted to the physical and cognitive development of teenagers.
- **Incremental Progression**: Students master foundational skills before advancing to more complex techniques.
- **Scenario‑Based Learning**: Realistic role‑playing to reinforce awareness and de‑escalation tactics.
- **Parental Involvement**: Regular updates and guidance for parents to support their teen’s development.

## Repository Purpose

This repository serves as the version‑controlled source for all teaching materials, lesson plans, and auxiliary documents. It enables:

- Consistent delivery of the curriculum across different instructors and locations.
- Collaborative refinement of drills and explanations.
- Transparent tracking of updates and improvements over time.

## How to Use This Repository

- Each module resides in its own directory, containing detailed notes, drill descriptions, and supporting resources.
- Issues are used to track development tasks, feedback, and suggested modifications.
- Releases correspond to stable versions of the curriculum, ready for classroom use.
